About Me

I am a 17-year old French American girl, born and raised in California. I live in Silver Lake, a tightly-knit neighborhood of Los Angeles, with my parents, 15-year old sister, 10-year old brother and two adorable cats. My siblings and I live in a bilingual household, with my French mother speaking exclusively french to us, and my American father speaking exclusively english to us. Growing up in a bicultural home has deeply shaped my personal identity. For example, it has inspired me to be bold and live abroad in France for my sophomore school year.

Mentoring & Volunteer Work

School on Wheels, Tutor

School on Wheels (SOW) is a non-profit organization that trains and matches volunteer tutors with homeless and low-income students. Currently, I am tutoring two young students in math and ELA online once a week each. Tutoring with SOW involves administering diagnostic quizzes, creating a lesson plan and preparing materials before each session.

MedSquared, Facilitator & Webmaster

MedSquared was a summer intensive program founded by four of my peers. Its purpose was to provide students interested in the medical field with relevant educational opportunities including Q&As with professionals and key resources. My role was to serve as a facilitator. I led group discussions during our weekly sessions, conducted multiple of the interviews with professionals and built and maintained the MedSquared website.

Los Angeles Public Library Teen Leadership Council, Teen Volunteer

The LAPL Teen Leadership Council, also known as Teens Leading Change (TLC), exists to give teenagers an opportunity to do a yearly creative community service project in the Silver Lake area. Once a week for 1-2 hours, our group would meet up to delegate tasks, review our individual progress from the week before, and plan out the remainder of our project. Our central theme was environmental activism. Initially, our intention was to host workshops oriented towards local businesses addressing the reasons and proper etiquette for recycling. We gathered funding for recycling bins to give away, wrote informational pamphlets, and designed an appropriate logo. Our hope was that if local businesses dealt with trash properly, we could improve the overall state of pollution in our neighborhood. Unfortunately, when COVID-19 hit and social distancing was imposed, hosting workshops was no longer a feasible option. We had to completely reconsider what type of project we could do. Ultimately, my group wrote and published a free children's book with the underlying purpose of informing younger children about recycling. Once we finished, we gave a live presentation to other library branches about our work. Mock Trial Club, Co-Founder & Co-President

In the summer before ninth grade, I attended the UCLA Mock Trial Summer Institute. This program focused on teaching the basics of the US legal system in the form of lectures and a final exam. Students were also expected to participate in a mock trial simulation. Participating in the Mock Trial Institute broadened my understanding and interest in our government system. Therefore, after both attending this summer program, my friend and I decided to co-found our own Mock Trial club at John Marshall High School. We designed flyers and hosted an informational session to promote our club. Once we had committed members, we held weekly meetings transferring our newfound knowledge and held our own in-class simulation.

Volley-Ball

Since middle school, I have played club volleyball at San Gabriel Elite. Once I entered high school I joined my schools' teams as well. My volleyball schedule has involved 3-5 school volleyball practices per week, 2 club practices per week, and 2 club clinics every weekend in addition to monthly club tournaments and weekly school matches during each season. In my opinion, the most important element of volleyball is teamwork. You can have the most skilled players in the world, but if they are unable to coordinate with each other effectively, their talent cannot be exploited. Playing volleyball for years has taught me important communication skills and the ability to work as a team.
